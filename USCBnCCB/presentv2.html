What is Core Banking?

Plain‑English definition for business leaders

System of Record

Houses customer & account data (CIF), balances, product setups.

Daily Processing

Posts transactions, calculates interest & fees, applies product rules.

Operational Controls

End‑of‑day, reconciliations, status & exception handling.

Financial & Reporting

Generates ledgers, statements, and daily activity reports.

Scope of Core Banking

What it must do consistently and at scale

Customer & Account Housing
Single source of truth for customers, accounts, products.
Interest & Fee Calculations
Accruals, postings, back‑dated adjustments.
Daily Activity Reporting
Statements, activity, exception & audit logs.
Financial Functions
General ledger feeds, settlements, reconciliations.

Use Case: Pricing & Billing

From analytics to posting

StepWho/LayerActionInterfaces
1. Pricing Analytics Pricing Service Queries user/account data; fetches Data Blocks from Operational Data layer; computes pricing; binds to pricing plan. Data Layer → Ops DB
2. Plan Finalization Pricing Service Applies business rules and approval workflow. API Layer (internal)
3. Billing Execution Billing Service Attaches account info; posts charges to core. Posting API (via API Layer)
4. Monitoring Billing / Ops Tracks posting outcomes and exceptions. TransactionStatus API
5. Reporting Reporting Layer Generates pricing & billing performance reports from Ops DB. Ops DB → Reports
