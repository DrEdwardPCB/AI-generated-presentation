üí° Updated Use Case: Digital Transfers for Canadians

Observation: Not all major Canadian banks allow customers to initiate wires digitally‚Äîsome require branch visits. Digital ‚Äúinternational money transfer‚Äù options often rely on third-party networks (e.g., Visa Direct, Western Union), with multi-day settlement and opaque routing.

Stablecoin Alternative: Bank-led on/off-ramp + stablecoin rails (USDC/PYUSD/etc.) enabling near-instant, low-fee transfers, transparent tracking, and programmable receivables.

  • Today‚Äôs rails: Banks frequently partner with centralized intermediaries (e.g., Western Union, Visa Direct; Wise as an alternative) ‚Äî adding black-box routing and fees.
  • Fee model signals: Network costs can be cents or fractions thereof; crypto processors commonly charge about 1% (sometimes plus a tiny fixed fee), allowing banks to price below wires yet capture more margin.
  • Deposit retention: Faster in-app settlement + competitive pricing encourages customers to keep funds within Scotiabank, lifting balances and cross-sell touchpoints.
